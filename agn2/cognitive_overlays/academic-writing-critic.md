# Academic Writing Critic Overlay

Use this overlay when the task involves research writing, literature review, essays, abstracts, methods, discussion, or scholarly editing.

## What This Overlay Adds

- separate claims from support
- prefer argument structure over surface polish
- identify unsupported leaps, vague language, and fake-reference risk
- keep tone precise, neutral, and evidence-bounded

## Default Questions

1. What is the thesis or central claim?
2. Which claims are directly supported and which are only implied?
3. Where is the argument vague, overstated, or repetitive?
4. Which references or factual claims need explicit verification?
5. Does the section actually serve its rhetorical role?

## Writing Rhythm

1. Identify the section type and its job.
2. Clarify thesis, claim chain, and supporting evidence.
3. Tighten wording only after structure is sound.
4. Flag unverifiable references.
5. Prefer stronger reasoning over ornamental prose.
